    \textbf{What the panels reveal:}
    \begin{itemize}
        \item \textbf{Ev2 — Fiedler vector ($\lambda=0.530$):} Optimal binary graph cut. Cleanly separates \textbf{coalition from opposition}. The large spectral gap ($\lambda_1\!\approx\!0 \to \lambda_2\!=\!0.530$) confirms a structurally sharp, stable divide.
        \item \textbf{Ev3 ($\lambda=0.946$):} Intra-bloc sub-structure — within the coalition, Lewica and Razem separate from the KO/PSL-TD/Polska2050 core.
        \item \textbf{Ev4 ($\lambda=0.995$):} Near-disconnected sub-communities — individual party identities emerge. KO, PiS, and Konfederacja become isolated islands, confirming that \textbf{party identity is secondary} to the coalition--opposition axis.
        \item \textit{``The graph's own geometry recovers the political divide — no labels needed.''}
    \end{itemize}
